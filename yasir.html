Gulmarg

Gulmarg is a beautiful hill station located in the Pir Panjal Range of the Himalayas, at an altitude of 2,650 m (8,694 ft), 56 km from Srinagar. It is known for its stunning natural beauty, with lush green meadows, snow-capped mountains, and picturesque valleys. The town has a rich history, dating back to the British era, when it was a popular destination for military men on leave. Today, Gulmarg is a popular ski resort, with the Institute of Skiing and Mountaineering established in 1968. The town has also hosted several national and international winter sports events, including the National Winter Games. In addition to its natural beauty and sporting facilities, Gulmarg is also home to several historic landmarks, including the Maharaja's palace, the English church, and the Nedou's hotel.

Sonmarg

Sonmarg, which means "Meadow of Gold", is a picturesque hill station located in the Ganderbal district of Jammu and Kashmir, India. Situated at an altitude of 2,740 meters (9,000 ft) above sea level, it is about 80 km northeast of Srinagar. Sonmarg is known for its breathtaking natural beauty, with lush green meadows, snow-capped mountains, and the majestic River Sindh flowing through it. The town is a popular base for treks to the nearby Himalayan peaks, including the Amarnath Temple, a sacred Hindu shrine. Sonmarg is also famous for its Thajiwas Glacier, which is a popular spot for horse riding and sledge riding. The town's scenic beauty, adventure opportunities, and spiritual significance make it a popular tourist destination in Kashmir.

Nishat Park

Nishat Park is a beautiful Mughal-era garden located on the banks of the Dal Lake in Srinagar, Jammu and Kashmir, India. Built in 1633 by Asif Khan, the brother of Nur Jahan, it is one of the largest and most stunning gardens in Kashmir. The park is designed in the traditional Mughal style, with a central water channel, fountains, and ornamental pools surrounded by lush green lawns, flowerbeds, and towering trees. The garden is divided into 12 terraces, each representing a different zodiac sign, and features a variety of flowers, including roses, lilies, and tulips. Nishat Park offers breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains and the Dal Lake, making it a popular tourist destination and a favorite spot for picnics and relaxation. It is also a popular spot for photography, with its picturesque beauty and serene atmosphere.
